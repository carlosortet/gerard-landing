FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is GERARD?

GERARD (Generative Research And Rapid Development) is an AI-powered R&D platform that combines synthetic persona simulation, multi-agent research orchestration, and the GEORadar intelligence engine to deliver enterprise-grade market research 10x faster and at 10x lower cost than traditional methods.

How does GERARD relate to GEORadar?

GERARD is built on top of 498AS's GEORadar platform, which monitors 15+ LLM models and provides 8 proprietary metrics for Generative Engine Optimization. GERARD extends this capability into market research, competitive intelligence, and synthetic persona simulation.

Are synthetic personas reliable?

Yes. GERARD's synthetic personas are not random AI generation—they're validated against real demographic data and behavioral patterns. Every synthetic insight includes a confidence score, and we offer hybrid validation with real sample confirmation for critical decisions.

Is GERARD GDPR compliant?

Absolutely. GERARD is EU-hosted (AWS Frankfurt), GDPR-compliant by design, with PII redaction capabilities and alignment with EU AI Act requirements. We serve regulated industries including banking and healthcare.

What's the typical project cost?

Projects range from €4K-€25K depending on scope, compared to $50K-$200K for traditional research firms. We also offer monthly subscriptions starting at €1,500/month for ongoing research needs.

How fast can I get results?

Most research projects deliver validated insights within 24-48 hours. Complex, multi-phase studies may take 3-5 days. Compare this to the 4-8 weeks typical of traditional research firms.

Can GERARD integrate with our existing tools?

Yes. GERARD offers REST API access, Slack integration for alerts, PDF report generation, and can export to common BI tools. Enterprise customers get dedicated integration support.

Who is 498AS?

498AS is the Barcelona-based technology company behind GERARD, GEORadar, and the complete GEO Suite. We serve enterprise clients including major Spanish banks, media companies, and retail brands.
